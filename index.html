About Us

The Global Distance Learning Association (GDLA) is a private, independent association dedicated to regulating and advocating for excellence in distance education worldwide.

Headquarters: Sydney, Australia
Europe Hub: Near Kärntner Ring, 1010 Vienna, Austria

Mission: Empowering standards, promoting best practices, and uniting stakeholders across the distance learning sector.
Vision: To be the trusted voice and quality benchmark in global distance learning.

Accreditation Process

GDLA supports institutions through a transparent, step-by-step accreditation journey to help ensure quality and credibility in distance education.

  1. Eligibility Check: Confirm that your institution primarily operates in distance education and meets GDLA's baseline standards.
  2. Registration & Application: Submit an online application with institutional information and required documentation.
  3. Self-Assessment: Complete a comprehensive self-study or checklist documenting your quality measures.
  4. Review & Site Visit: Undergo desk review, potential peer evaluation, and virtual or in-person site visits.
  5. Final Decision & Reporting: Receive an accreditation report, respond to recommendations, and get official recognition when approved.

A detailed accreditation handbook and guidelines are provided to member institutions to navigate each step effectively.

Norms

  • Integrity and Transparency: Members must adhere to ethical practices in administration, teaching, and communications.
  • Learner Protection: Commitment to clear communication of program expectations and protection of learner rights.
  • Data Privacy: Compliance with international data protection regulations safeguarding personal information.
  • Quality Monitoring: Ongoing self-assessment and adherence to GDLA's rigorous standards and guidelines.

Terms & Conditions

  • Membership Eligibility: Open to qualifying institutions, technology providers, and policy organizations engaged in distance learning.
  • Use of Accreditation Seal: Restricted to fully accredited members; unauthorized use is prohibited.
  • Renewal & Compliance: Accreditation is valid for five years; requires annual reporting and compliance audits.
  • Dispute Resolution: Formal mediation handled by GDLA’s Ethics Committee addresses member grievances.
  • Intellectual Property: GDLA resources are protected and reproduction without permission is forbidden.
